This is an ongoing English translation, annotated for readers who want to understand not just the stories — but the world that produced them.

About this project

Notes from the Thatched Study (阅微草堂笔记, Yuèwēi Cǎotáng Bǐjì) is a five-part collection of supernatural and moral tales compiled by Ji Yun (纪昀, courtesy name Xiaolan 晓岚) between 1789 and 1798. As Grand Secretary and chief editor of the Siku Quanshu — the most ambitious library project in Chinese history — Ji Yun had access to scholars and officials across the empire, and he treated their strange anecdotes with the same documentary care he brought to imperial scholarship.

Where his contemporary Pu Songling (author of Strange Tales from a Chinese Studio) wrote elaborate, plot-driven supernatural fiction, Ji Yun wrote sparingly — often in fewer than 200 characters — and let his observations speak for themselves. The result is a book that reads as both ghost-story collection and ethnographic record of late Qing life.
